Welcome

I am a Ph.D. student in the Department of Computer Science at Purdue University. I am fortunate to work under the supervision of Prof. Dan Goldwasser. Broadly speaking, my research interests include Natural Language Understanding and Computational Social Science. Currently, I am working towards building a framework for analyzing polarization in news media. In this work, instead of categorizing the entire news article to a specific ideology, we take a different approach to understand the narrative from a claim's perspective.

Prior to this, I received an M.S degree in Computer Science and Engineering from The Pennsylvania State University, USA in 2021. At Penn State, I worked at the interface between computer science and economics. Specifically, we designed a synthetic prediction market to assess the credibility of published claims in the social and behavioral sciences literature (as part of the DARPA SCORE Project).

Before pursuing graduate studies, I worked as a Data Scientist at MiQ, a digital advertising firm, where I developed solutions to estimate the best match between advertisers and internet users with economic constraints. I received my B.Tech. degree in Computer Science and Engineering from PES University, India, in 2018.
